Kopi is one of those everyday prices that quietly shapes what people think a normal day should cost.

When something sits inside ritual, price changes feel moral before they feel economic. A small increase can trigger more emotion than a much larger expense elsewhere, because the purchase is repeated, visible, and shared.

This draft is where I want to unpack the gap between perceived affordability and actual margin pressure.
