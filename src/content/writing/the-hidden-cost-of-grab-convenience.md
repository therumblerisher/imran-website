Convenience is never just a price on a screen.

It changes habits, compresses patience, and teaches people to value speed differently. Once a behaviour feels frictionless, the premium attached to it becomes easier to ignore.

I want this piece to sit somewhere between household budgeting, urban life, and platform economics.
